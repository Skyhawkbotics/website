Outreach Events

What have we done in the past, and what impact have we made?

Hawkbot1cs has participated in many outreach events. You can browse this list to see our past activities.

Note: The season dates are based on our seasons, not official FTC season dates.

DECODE

FLL Camp with Gearforward

Hillsboro Brookwood Library

July 23rd - August 13th, 2025

Hawkbot1cs and Gearforward are running a weekly FLL camp for younger students. The camp will teach students how to build and program robots using LEGO Mindstorms. Students will learn the basics of robotics, including sensors, motors, and programming, while also having fun and making new friends. This camp is a great opportunity for students to get hands-on experience with robotics and prepare for future competitions.

Online Autodesk Fusion Workshop

July 25th - 27th, 2025

Our team is hosting a FREE online Autodesk Fusion workshop to teach interested students how to use Autodesk Fusion. This workshop is designed for beginners and will cover the basics of CAD, including sketching, modeling, and joints. Students will learn how to create their own designs and gain valuable skills for future robotics projects.

Ashcreek-Crestwood Picnic

July 14th, 2025

We attended the Ashcreek-Crestwood Picnic to promote STEM education and robotics. We showcased our new candy shooter robot and engaged with students and parents, inspiring the next generation of engineers.

MPACT Electronics Drive

Al's Garden & Home

April 26th, 2025

We partnered with MPACT to host an electronics drive at Al's Garden & Home. We collected old electronics, took them apart, and recycled them. We also helped take apart decommissioned laptops from the Beaverton School District. This event helped raise awareness about e-waste and its environmental impact, while also supporting our local community.

Sexton Mountain Science Fair

Sexton Mountain Elementary School

April 17th, 2025

We attended the Sexton Mountain Science Fair to promote STEM education and robotics. We showcased our INTO THE DEEP robot, allowed students to drive our new drivetrain, and engaged with students and parents, inspiring the next generation of engineers.
